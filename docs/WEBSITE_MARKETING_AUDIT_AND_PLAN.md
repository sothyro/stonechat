**Stonechat Communications** — Commercial-grade content, flow, and conversion improvements while keeping the current visual style and color scheme.

## 1. Refined Sitemap & Page Roles

| Route | Page role | Primary goal |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| `/` | **Home** | Orient visitors, present three pillars (apps, training, publishing), drive to Consultations or Contact |
| `/about` | **About us** | Build trust: who we are, what we do, why choose us. CTA: Contact / Get a quote |
| `/journey` | **Our story** | Deeper narrative, methodology, training & publishing. CTA: Events, Apps, Contact |
| `/events` | **Workshops & events** | List upcoming trainings, register. CTA: Book seat, Consultations |
| `/apps` | **Apps & store** | Showcase app development, clinic app, book store. CTA: Get a quote, Add to cart |
| `/book` | **Publications** | Book store focus (if separate from /apps) or redirect. CTA: Add to cart, Contact |
| `/consultations` | **Get a quote / Book consultation** | Service selection → date/time → details → confirm. CTA: Confirm & book |
| `/contact` | **Contact** | Form + office info + WhatsApp/email. CTA: Submit message |

**Navigation (recommended):**

- **Header:** Home | **About** | Training ▾ (Events, Our Story, On the news) | Apps | Publications | Consultations | [Contact Us]
- **Footer:** Keep Quick Links + Apps & Store; add **About** to Quick Links if not in header.
- **Drawer:** Add **About** as first item after Home (or under a “Company” group with Our Story).

**Gap fixed:** The About page (`/about`) currently has **no link in header or footer**. Visitors cannot discover it. Add “About” to the main nav and footer.

## 2. Content Audit Summary

### 2.1 Clarity & redundancy

| Location | Issue | Recommendation |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| **Hero** | Headline is app-only (“Quality apps…”, “Apps & digital solutions studio”). Business also does training and publishing. | Either (a) broaden hero to “Quality apps, training & publishing—without breaking the bank” and subline “Apps, workshops & books—all in one place,” or (b) keep app focus and add one line: “Plus communication training and book publishing.” |
| **Events section** | Heading uses “Clean architectures. Elegant interfaces. Low cost.” — that’s the **apps** message, not events. | Replace with events-focused headline, e.g. “Hands-on workshops. Clear skills. Real impact.” |
| **Academies (Knowledge) section** | Body repeats hero/app copy (“modern, beautiful, affordable apps…”). Section is about **training**. | Shorten body to training benefits and outcomes; remove duplicate app feature list. |
| **Consultations section** | Heading “You don’t need jargon. You need a partner.” is good but generic. Cards are service-specific. | Add a short intro line: “Choose a service below. We’ll match you with the right option and next steps.” Keep heading. |
| **Story section** | “Who we are” + three paragraphs repeat the same value props (apps, languages, turnaround). | Keep one short “who we are” paragraph; second paragraph = proof (languages, speed, tone); third = invitation (“Get in touch”). Reduce repetition with About/Journey. |
| **About page** | Very thin: 4 bullets only. No CTA. | Add one short paragraph after bullets (“We’re here to help…”). Add CTA button: “Get a quote” or “Contact us.” Add **Featured in** only if you have real logos; else remove or replace with “Trusted by teams across Cambodia and beyond.” |
| **Journey page** | Story blocks duplicate Home + About. Period 9 & Phoenix are good differentiators. | Keep Journey as the “long story”; trim repeated sentences so each page has a distinct role. |
| **Contact intro** | Good. | Optionally add: “We usually reply within 1 business day.” |

### 2.2 Messaging consistency

- **One-liner for the whole site:**  
  “Stonechat Communications: quality apps, communication training, and book publishing—clear, human, and affordable.”
- Use “we” and “you” consistently; lead with **benefits** (what the client gets), then support with features (platforms, languages, speed).
- **Trust:** Add one line near contact/consultation: “Trusted by businesses and individuals in Cambodia and beyond” (or a concrete number, e.g. “50+ projects delivered”) if accurate.

### 2.3 Marketing strength

- **Headlines:** Each section should have one clear headline that states benefit or outcome (e.g. “Hands-on workshops. Clear skills. Real impact.” for events).
- **CTAs:** Use action-oriented, specific labels: “Get a quote”, “Book your seat”, “Contact us”, “Request a consultation”, “Add to cart”.
- **Trust:** Testimonials are in place; consider a short “Why choose us” strip (e.g. 3 bullets: Clear communication, Fast turnaround, Multilingual) on Home or About.
